Where Were Ceballos and Gabriel vs. Liverpool? Squad Rotation/Tactics?

Most gooners were very surprised to see our summer-signing Gabriel left on the bench in favor of David Luiz. Gabriel has been exceptional in his first two premier league starts and even leads the league in passing accuracy for a centerback.

With that in mind, it becomes very confusing how Arteta favored David Luiz. Dani Ceballos was also left out of the squad in favor of Elneny. Usely this would ensure more defensive stability, but I'm willing to argue it wasn't worth it against Liverpool as it drained creativity on the ball. Liverpool are most dangerous down the wings with their impressive fullbacks/wingers with attacks rarely coming straight through the middle. I feel Dani has held his own in becoming a team player willing to do his work defensively. His creativity was sorely missed and could be seen as soon as he came on.

Gunners Come Up Short Against Champions at Anfield

Decent effort from our boys today, but an overall dominant performance from the champions. Liverpool's quality down the flanks really proved vital as that's where all the danger came from.

The gunners did not have much ball possession at all, patiently waiting for a chance to get the ball and counter. Unfortunately, when the opportunities arose we needed more creativity or quality in the pass as the final ball was there against Liverpool's high line. Lacazette scored early on, but we'll all agree he will be kicking himself at not finishing his one0-on-one opportunity.

I am also concerned with how some of our players are sticking with playing out of the back when they would be much better served booting the ball out of there. Against a side like Liverpool, it would take several great first-time/quick passes to break through for a chance at a counter. Overall, wish arteta was a bit more brave in his tactics as Leeds were on opening day.

Nketiah Scores the Winner Against West Ham

Well, we got the win today, but boy was it a nervous match. West Ham looked dangerous on their counters, but luckily could not convert more than one goal.

Gabriel was truly tested today, and I think he'll take it as good experience for the pace of a Premier League game. Holding was decent, but I can't help feeling nervous when an attacker goes at him with pace.

The main issue today was that our midfield could not prevent the counter attacks. This is where I've been crying out (for what feels like forever) for a world class defensive midfielder. Partey is the man and would provide what Fabinho provides to a championship side in Liverpool.

In the end some magic from Saka made it easy for ceballos to center it to Nketiah for the winner. Happy as always for the three points.

Would Transfer Window Be a Bust Without Partey or Auoar?

With the transfer window nearing its close, many gooners are bracing themselves hoping for at least one more summer signing. Most are hoping its either defensive midfielder Partey from Athletico Madrid or attacking midfielder Aouar from Lyon. In this article I'd like to bring up the benefits of either one (ideally we'd get both of course!).

Let's begin with Aouar from Lyon. He's 22 years old and full of potential. He offers a quick transition from defense to offense and great close control. He would definitely add some much needed creativity to our midfield. Currently I'd say he's most similar to Ceballos, but Dani has proven he's willing to work defensively for the team in the current shape Arteta has us playing in. At Lyon, Aouar had a bit more freedom to drive the ball forward, but did show he's capable of staying deeper for the team when needed.

Partey is 27 years old and to me, would offer more to the team. Partey wins the ball back tremendously well for Athletico and also has driving runs/great distribution to attacking players. He covers ground very well and would allow Xhaka to be in better positions to use his great left foot to find our front three.

Arsenal Sign Gabriel from Lille for $34.7m

Gabriel has officially signed for Arsenal from Lille for a reported fee of $34.7m.

The Brazilian will be a major addition to a team lacking defensive depth and quality. Gabriel is good in the air and does not go to ground to quickly. He's very patient, but effective in making his tackles.

Eventually I think we all would like to see a Saliba/Gabriel partnership, but I think Saliba needs more time to develop. Good thing is both are young, so things look brighter than previous years for our defence.

Arsenal Sign Willian From Chelsea On a Free Transfer

Willian has officially signed for Arsenal from Chelsea on a free transfer.

The Brazilian will add some experience and depth to our front line. He may be 32, but he was arguably Chelsea's most influential player last season for attacking moves.

The move also brings Pepe's starting spot into question. Pepe has shown quality at times, but still lacks the consistency we need. With Willian for competition, hopefully Pepe will step it up and reach his potential.

If not, Willian will take care of business in the meantime.
